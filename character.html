Who Is Persephone?

PERSEPHONE, the goddess queen of the underworld and the wife of the God Hades. She is also known as the goddess of spring growth and is worshipped by agricultural-based cults that were promised a blessful afterlife.

Persephone, also called Kore or Cora, is the daughter of Zeus and Demeter. She is oftern depicted as a beautiful young goddess who emobides both the life and death and also rebirth. She is mostly symbolized by the natural cycle of life and death but she has many recognized symboles.

Her symbols include:

  • Pomegranate
  • Seeds of Grain
  • Torch
  • Flowers
  • Deer

Her childrens:

  • Melinoe
  • Dionysus
  • Erinyes

Her Personality

Being the embodiment of both life and death, her personality is that of dual nature. She is the daughter of Demeter, which embodies innocence and renewal of life in spring. However, being the Queen of the Underworld, she has a darker and more solemn aspect that associates with death and afterlife.

Persephone stands as a bridge between life and death and holds the role of a mediator between realms of the living and the dead.
